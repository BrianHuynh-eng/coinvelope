Before you do that, though, let us tell you more about envelope budgeting.

Envelope budgeting is a straightforward yet powerful method that offers visual control by dividing your expenses into categories, making it easier to track and stick to your budget. Unlike traditional methods that rely on percentages of your income, envelope budgeting enforces spending limits, preventing overspending once the money in a particular envelope is gone. This customizable approach allows you to tailor categories like 'Rent,' 'Utilities,' 'Entertainment,' and 'Savings' to fit your specific financial needs, promoting financial discipline and helping you stay within your means. For example, consider envelopes for 'Groceries' ($200), 'Rent' ($1,500), 'Utilities' ($150), 'Entertainment' ($300), 'Transportation' ($500), 'Savings' ($500), and 'Health & Wellness' ($100).

Envelope budgeting is more "applied" and "tangible" than the traditional method. By using this system, you gain better control over your finances, ensure you stay within your budget, and make conscious spending decisions, ultimately fostering a healthier financial mindset.
