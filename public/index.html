Before you do that, though, let us tell you more about envelope budgeting.

Envelope budgeting is a powerful way of managing your spending by dividing your monthly income into separate pieces. Each piece is stored inside of an envelope, and each piece is given a category of spending. Traditional budgeting chops up your income into percentages and allocates those percentages on a category of spending. This, however, is very abstract. Because the traditional method uses percentages, people will feel less guilty of spending over that category budget. Envelope budgeting solves this problem. Once the money in an envelope is gone and "spent up", you cannot spend anything on that category again. Now, it doesn't actually force you to stop spending on that category. Rather, it psychologically forces you to stop spending because you are more likely to look at a stash of cash draining out, than a bunch of percentages thrown at you. Envelope budgeting forces you to enter the EXACT dollar amount of that category, making you feel guilty for every dollar you spend (as it should be!)

For example, some categories are: 'Rent,' 'Utilities,' 'Entertainment,' and 'Savings'. And maybe, you can set the budget for each category as: 'Groceries' ($200), 'Rent' ($1,500), 'Utilities' ($150), 'Entertainment' ($300), 'Transportation' ($500), 'Savings' ($500), and 'Health & Wellness' ($100). It's that easy!

The normal way of budgeting with envelopes is to use physical envelopes, stuff cash in, and take them out and use that cash to pay for whatever you spend it on. This is great. However, we cannot stuff ten thousand dollars in one envelope. Coinvelope is the digital version of envelope budgeting. Now, let's forget about the old school stuff. Let's make budgeting less abstract and more applied!
